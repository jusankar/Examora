## Question
Read the following extract from the poem \"The Road Not Taken\" by Robert Frost and answer the questions that follow:

*\"Two roads diverged in a yellow wood,  
And sorry I could not travel both  
And be one traveler, long I stood  
And looked down one as far as I could  
To where it bent in the undergrowth;\"*

1. What decision is the speaker grappling with in this extract?  
2. How does the imagery of the 'yellow wood' contribute to the theme of choice?

## Marks
4 Marks

## Expected Answer Points
1. **Decision**: The speaker is faced with a choice between two paths or directions in life, symbolizing significant life decisions.
2. **Imagery Contribution**: The 'yellow wood' suggests a sense of autumn, symbolizing change and the idea of transition. It sets a contemplative mood, highlighting the importance of the choice being made.

## Marking Scheme Guidance
- Award 2 marks for a clear explanation of the decision being made. (1 mark for identifying the choice; 1 mark for the significance of being unable to travel both paths).
- Award 2 marks for analyzing the imagery. (1 mark for identifying 'yellow wood' as a symbol of change; 1 mark for discussing its effect on the overall theme of choice).
